With more than 180 physicians on faculty in the Department of Medicine, our faculty to resident ratio is never larger than 1:4.

The Texas A&M Health Science Center College of Medicine - Scott & White three-year residency in Internal Medicine is fully accredited by the Accreditation Council for Graduate Medical Education (ACGME).

Accrediting agencies have been impressed at the breadth of subspecialty education we offer. Our curriculum is designed to prepare well-trained internists who can go into primary care, additional subspecialty training or academic medicine.

We accept 20 categorical internal medicine residents and six preliminary positions per year.

Clinical Rotations:
Our program is designed to provide residents well-rounded training that balances the many aspects of internal medicine:

  • Inpatient versus outpatient care
  • Primary care versus critical care
  • General medicine versus subspecialty care

More than one-third of the rotations focus on outpatient ambulatory care, which is a vital aspect of both primary care and subspecialty care and where a significant amount of time is spent treating chronic diseases.

During ambulatory care training, residents develop a "panel" of patients they will follow throughout the residency to cultivate strong personal and professional relationships with patients. During this time, residents are also encouraged to learn important skills, such as billing and coding, time management and office procedures.

We also offer extensive subspecialty rotations which enable residents to focus their study with an expert in that field. Some of our subspecialty rotations include:

  • Neurology
  • Geriatrics
  • Gynecology
  • Psychiatry
  • Dermatology
  • Ophthalmology
  • Orthopedics

Electives time is also available for residents to gain additional skills or perform research to further their education based on their personal long-term career goals.

Procedural Skills:
During rotations, each specialty area includes its own set of procedural instruction, including indications, complications and techniques. We have a formal central-line training program for all residents. Procedural training computer aids are also available for review.

Residents also spend time at our Clinical Simulation Center, where residents have an opportunity to rehearse and master skills in controlled environments where patients are never at risk.

Call Schedule:

Our department is committed to following the ACGME duty hour regulations while maximizing the learning experience for residents on call. Residents are allowed to alternate weekend responsibilities so that they average at least one day out of seven outside of the hospital.

Our ICU night float rotation is also designed to comply with the duty hour policy. We have many non-teaching inpatient medicine teams staffed only by hospitalists to comply with RRC-IM admission limits.

Clinical Rotations:
During the first year, residents rotate on a 4-week block schedule. Rotations include:

  • Scott & White wards (four blocks)
  • VA wards
  • Cardiology
  • Nephrology
  • VA urgent care
  • ER
  • MICU
  • CVICU
  • Elective
  • Night float

Call Schedule:
Residents will have two 2-week blocks of night float, which includes taking house offer calls (no admissions) on patients admitted to the teaching teams. Residents will also have three blocks of night medicine, which entails arriving at 8 p.m., admitting up to five patients and rounding in the early morning with the team.

Clinical Rotations:
Upper levels also rotate on a 4-week block schedule and alternate will the PGY-1 every two weeks. Rotations include:

  • Scott & White wards
  • VA wards
  • Night float
  • Medicine consult
  • Gastroenterology
  • Oncology
  • Geriatrics
  • Infectious disease
  • Ambulatory care
  • VA MICU
  • Elective
  • Inpatient selective
  • Outpatient selective

Call Schedule:
Call includes three blocks of every fourth night (q4) and one block of ICU night float split into two half-month blocks.

Clinical Rotations:
Upper levels also rotate on a 4-week block schedule and alternate will the PGY-1 every two weeks. Rotations include:

  • Scott & White wards (two blocks)
  • MICU
  • Endocrinology
  • Rheumatology
  • Pulmonary
  • Neurology
  • Hematology
  • Electives (two blocks)
  • Outpatient selectives (two blocks)
  • Ambulatory care

Call Schedule:
During the third year, residents have no overnight call responsibilities to allow time for board preparation.

Third year residents will take call during weekday rotations: two early-call days, one mid-call day and one late-call day. Residents will also work one day each weekend.

Central Texas Veterans Health Care System (Temple, TX)

The Central Texas Veterans Health Care System (CTVHCS) is one of the largest VA medical consortiums in the Unites States. It comprises one of the newest VA medical/surgical hospitals in the country (Olin E. Teague Veterans' Center in Temple), a large / inpatient and outpatient psychiatric facility in Waco, several VA nursing home facilities, and outpatient clinics throughout Central Texas.

The Teague Center has been a principal teaching campus for the College of Medicine since the college's inception.
